Treatment Overview

Total knee replacement, also known as total knee arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure in which the worn or damaged surfaces of the knee joint are removed and replaced with artificial parts.

This procedure is typically recommended for patients with severe arthritis or significant knee injury that causes persistent pain and limits daily activities.

  • Advanced surgical techniques
  • Experienced orthopedic surgeons
  • State-of-the-art medical facilities
  • Comprehensive post-operative care

Eligibility for Treatment

You may be a candidate for total knee replacement if you have:

  • Severe knee pain that limits everyday activities
  • Moderate to severe knee pain while resting
  • Chronic knee inflammation and swelling
  • Knee deformity
  • Failure to obtain pain relief from medications

About Treatment

The knee replacement procedure typically takes 1-2 hours and involves:

1

Anesthesia

General or spinal anesthesia is administered

2

Incision

A surgical incision is made over the knee

3

Replacement

Damaged bone and cartilage are replaced with artificial components

4

Closure

The incision is closed and bandaged

Treatment FAQs

How long does knee replacement surgery take?

The surgery typically takes 1-2 hours, depending on the complexity of the case.

What is the recovery time after knee replacement?

Most patients can return to normal activities within 3-6 months, with full recovery taking up to a year.

Are there any risks involved in knee replacement surgery?

Like any surgery, there are risks, but serious complications are rare when performed by experienced surgeons.
